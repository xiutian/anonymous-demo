Neuron-Level Emotion Control in Speech-Generative Large Audio-Language Models

Anonymous submission to Interspeech 2026

Abstract

Large audio-language models (LALMs) can produce expressive speech, yet reliable emotion control remains elusive: conversions often miss the target affect and may degrade linguistic fidelity through refusals, hallucinations, or paraphrase. We present the first neuron-level study of emotion control in speech-generative LALMs and demonstrate that compact emotion-sensitive neurons (ESNs) are causally actionable, enabling training-free emotion steering at inference time. ESNs are identified via success-filtered activation aggregation enforcing both emotion realization and content preservation. Across three LALMs (Qwen2.5-Omni-7B, MiniCPM-o 4.5, Kimi-Audio), ESN interventions yield emotion-specific gains that generalize to unseen speakers and are supported by automatic and human evaluation. Controllability depends on selector design, mask sparsity, filtering, and intervention strength. Our results establish a mechanistic framework for training-free emotion control in speech generation.

Speech Samples

This demo complements the main paper by providing direct audio examples that illustrate the effects of neuron-level intervention in speech-generative LALMs. Starting from a neutral source utterance, we instruct the model to repeat the content faithfully while expressing a target emotion. We then compare the model's original output with outputs obtained after intervening on a compact set of emotion-sensitive neurons (ESNs), identified from successful emotional voice conversion examples using different selectors. We include both successful cases and representative failure modes. The sections below present (1) baseline-versus-intervened comparisons across identification methods, and (2) comparisons across intervention strengths (α). For comparability, we keep the model, top rate, success set size, and intervention method fixed throughout (Qwen2.5-Omni-7B, r = 0.5%, c=50, targeted steering).

Part 2: Intervention Strength α

This section examines how the strength of the intervention changes the generated speech. For each emotion, we present four single samples: the baseline output, followed by outputs with α = 0.5, α = 1.0, and α = 2.0. Other settings are held constant (Qwen2.5-Omni-7B, r = 0.5%, c=50, selector: CAS). Here, α controls how strongly the identified emotion-sensitive neurons are steered at inference time. The expected pattern is a trade-off: increasing α can make the target emotion more pronounced, but overly strong intervention may also harm content preservation or introduce unnatural generations.
